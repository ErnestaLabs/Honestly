What each metric means

Built on HM Land Registry sold prices

The foundation of any honest valuation. HM Land Registry Price Paid Data is the definitive record of what homes actually sold for — not asking prices, not estimates, not guesses. Honestly pulls this data directly. Zoopla and Rightmove use it as one input among many. Estate agents may reference it but are not required to show it to you. Hometrack and Sprift build on it but gate it behind professional subscriptions.

You see the evidence behind the number

This is the core difference. Honestly shows you every figure and where it came from: the sold comparables, their prices, the adjustments for condition, the live market steer. You can check each one against the public record. Zoopla and Rightmove give you a number with no visible methodology. Estate agents tell you a figure without showing the sold evidence. Hometrack and Sprift show evidence — to professionals who pay hundreds of pounds a month.

No incentive to inflate the figure

Estate agents compete on valuation: the highest number often wins the instruction. That creates an incentive to inflate. Online estimate models are automated but have no accountability. Honestly has no instruction to win, no listing to land, and nothing to sell you on the back of a high number. The figure is whatever the evidence supports.

First report free

All consumer-facing options offer a free first step. The difference is what you get. An online estimate is a single number. An agent valuation is a sales conversation. An Honestly report is a full evidence trail with sold comparables, adjustments, and a PDF with a spoken walkthrough — free, no account, no card.

Ready in about 60 seconds

Honestly runs in about a minute. Online estimates are instant but opaque. An estate agent valuation takes days and requires a visit. Hometrack and Sprift are fast for professionals but inaccessible to consumers.

No account, no card, no sales call

Honestly runs on Telegram: no account to create, no password to remember, no card details requested. Your first report is free and there is no "a local expert will call you" waiting on the other side. Online estimates require sign-up for full access. Estate agent valuations require your contact details and will result in follow-up calls. Pro tools require paid accounts.
