**That contradiction is the finding:** the per-sqm method fails a basic sanity check for this property, because the underlying EPC floor areas are unreliable. When same-street, same-type £/sqm varies that widely, the metric cannot discriminate value at the individual-property level.

**Conclusion:** £/sqm is reported for completeness but is **not** used to set the valuation. The whole-price comparable evidence in §2 is the reliable basis. If a like-for-like £/sqm were nonetheless forced, the subject's £4,125/sqm at the offer sits *below* the same-street midpoint (≈ £5,450/sqm), but that comparison inherits the unreliable areas and should not be relied on.

2. **EPC floor areas are unreliable** at the individual-property level (building-level matches; duplicate areas across units; same-street £/sqm varying 49%). All £/sqm figures are indicative only and were **not** used to set the valuation. (§3)

If accurate, it supports the upper half of the range; if overstated, the lower half. An EPC/measured-survey check is advised before relying on size.
4. **Bedroom counts are incomplete** in the Land Registry/EPC data — the confirmed-3-bed sample (n=36) excludes 3-bed sales where the bedroom field was blank, so the 3-bed median is based on a subset, not all 3-bed transactions.
5. **No active terraced asking-price statistic available.** PropertyData's `/prices` returned "insufficient data" for the terraced filter in N22, so a clean live-market terraced asking figure could not be produced. Asking data (seller hopes) is in any case kept separate from sold evidence and was not blended into the valuation.
6. **Comparable window is 18 months** as specified; the freshest same-street sale is ~12 months old (May 2025), hence the explicit trend adjustment in §5 rather than treating it as current.
7. **Sample-size caution:** same-street evidence rests on only **two** transactions; the Tier-2 3-bed comps are robust (n=36 district-wide) but individual £/sqm and EPC areas within them are noisy. Conclusions are framed conservatively to under-claim rather than over-claim.

- 比卖方受邀的 "offers over £675,000" 底价低 **£15,000**（−2.2%）。
- **高于**混合卧室数的区段中位数（£615,000）——这对 3 卧而言符合预期。

**结论：** £660,000 是一个合理、有证据支撑、买方估价师可以站得住脚的报价——但**并非**强势或足额报价。卖方估价师可合理地坚持向 **£675,000–£694,000** 靠拢，理由包括同街 3 卧成交（趋势调整后约 £690,000）、3 卧全区中位数（£694,000），以及标的物业大于同类的楼面面积与第二个卫生间。现实的议价差距较窄（约 £15,000–£35,000）。无连环链（chain-free）对卖方而言是真实的非价格优势，理性上可支持为换取速度与确定性而接受靠近公允价值下限的价格——但这属于商业判断，而非价格证据本身所决定。

## 7. 局限与数据缺口

1. **2015 年成交价（£580,000）——未核实。** 超出 PropertyData 84 个月 Price Paid 窗口，且未提供门牌号以锁定记录。请通过 HM Land Registry Price Paid 按完整地址核实。（§4）
2. **EPC 楼面面积在单一物业层面不可靠**（建筑层级匹配；多单元共用同一面积；同街 £/平方米 相差 49%）。所有 £/平方米 数字仅作参考，**未**用于设定估值。（§3）
3. **标的物业自身楼面面积（160 平方米）未经独立确认。** 相对 N22 典型 3 卧排屋成交（数据中多为约 74–124 平方米）偏大。若属实，支持区间上半部；若高估，则支持下半部。在依赖面积前建议核对 EPC / 实测测量。
4. **卧室数在数据中不完整**——确认为 3 卧的样本（n=36）不含卧室字段为空的 3 卧成交，故 3 卧中位数基于子集，而非全部 3 卧交易。
5. **无可用的在售排屋要价统计。** PropertyData 的 `/prices` 对 N22 排屋筛选返回"数据不足"，故无法得出干净的在售排屋要价数字。要价数据（卖方期望）在任何情况下都与成交证据分开，未并入估值。
6. **可比窗口为 18 个月**（按要求）；最新的同街成交距今约 12 个月（25 年 5 月），故 §5 明确做了趋势调整，而非将其视为当前价。
7. **样本量警示：** 同街证据仅基于 **两** 笔交易；第二梯队 3 卧可比稳健（全区 n=36），但其中个别 £/平方米 与 EPC 面积存在噪声。结论以保守方式表述，宁可低估而不高估。
