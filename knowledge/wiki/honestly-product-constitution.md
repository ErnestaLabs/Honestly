## Core promise

> One address. A defended price. Sold evidence shown.

Honestly is a Transparent AVM: a valuation engine that gives a defended number, shows the evidence, explains the adjustment, and grades its own confidence.

## Operating rule

Every inch exists to convert or retain.

A feature must help one of these outcomes:

1. user trusts the first valuation faster
2. user understands the evidence behind a price
3. user takes a next action
4. user compares a property decision
5. user returns through alerts/watchlists/monitoring
6. user upgrades to decision intelligence

If it does not do one of those, cut it.

## Free value must be real

Free must include:

- fast valuation
- condition-adjusted range
- confidence
- proof rows
- plain-English explanation
- lightweight PDF evidence pack
- clear next action

Free is not a teaser made of public trivia. It is the trust event.

## Paid value

Paid sells decision intelligence, not public facts:

- mortgage and finance pressure
- down-valuation exposure
- pre-survey risk
- seller liquidity diagnosis
- compare homes
- watchlist/monitoring
- full decision pack
- partner/service choice later

## Public copy rules

Avoid internal/meta language. Public pages serve the user's decision, not our content strategy.

Banned public copy:

- reader / readers
- authority engine
- the product
- blog page
- How we did this
- How we built this
- Why compare on a blog page
- Because the blog
- internal/meta explanation of the publication strategy
- the word "working"

## Critical conversion constraints

- Never ask for floor area before first value.
- Never ask for income, deposit, mortgage term, EPC, tenure, or similar paid-module inputs before first value.
- Missing floor area affects confidence only.
- Floor area is our job to fetch or infer from public data, especially EPC.
- Public data must not become a form gate.
