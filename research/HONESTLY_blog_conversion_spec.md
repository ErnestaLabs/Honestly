# Honestly - blog conversion spec

## Purpose
The blog is the authority engine, SEO/AEO engine, and lead-routing engine.
It is not the product. The product is the Telegram-first appraisal and compare machine.

## What the blog must do
1. Attract high-intent search traffic.
2. Prove we know the market.
3. Route readers into Telegram, the Mini App, or compare service.
4. Capture email and Telegram leads.
5. Feed analytics and content planning.
6. Support recurring upsells after the first win.

## What the blog already does well
- Daily rebuilt postcode and city pages.
- Official source-backed sold prices.
- Asking vs sold separation.
- FAQ and Dataset schema.
- Free PDF lead gate.
- Telegram CTA.
- Social sentiment blocks labeled as sentiment only.
- Watch cards for buyer, seller, agent.

## Blog page types

### 1) City hub
Purpose: authority + navigation.
Examples: London, Manchester, Bristol.
CTA order:
1. Browse district reports
2. Open a district report
3. Telegram valuation CTA
4. Follow city updates on Telegram

### 2) District report
Purpose: convert intent from local search.
Includes:
- sold prices
- price per m²
- live asking prices
- days on market
- stuck stock
- rent / yield context where relevant
- area intelligence
- what people say
- FAQ
- source list

CTA order:
1. Value your own address on Telegram
2. Follow district updates on Telegram
3. Email me the free PDF
4. Read more free reports

### 3) Market commentary / index page
Purpose: authority and inbound links.
Use for:
- market explanations
- asking vs sold gaps
- city-centre or district-wide studies
- newsletters, blogs and publications people already read
- "why is this house not selling" style market logic
CTA order:
1. Run the free valuation
2. Compare options
3. Start watchlist or alerts
4. Download free report

### 4) Lead magnet page
Purpose: capture email while preserving free access.
Rules:
- core page stays free
- PDF is the gated extra
- persona field is required
- no hidden payment wall
- Telegram CTA remains visible

## Content clusters that should print money
- instant UK home valuation
- down-valued property
- why is this house not selling
- tools to help compare houses
- home affordability spreadsheet
- mortgage-ready comparison
- renovation ROI / value uplift
- alerts / watchlists / instant notifications
- seller timing / price drop reasons
- service-charge / leasehold / mortgage / broker / solicitor comparisons
- publication analysis / headlines vs the data

## CTA rules
### Always visible
- Telegram valuation CTA
- browse free reports CTA

### Contextual
- compare options CTA on comparison / decision posts
- watchlist / alerts CTA on market timing posts
- PDF lead gate on high-intent report pages
- broker / solicitor / surveyor compare CTA on friction posts

### Never hide
- the core answer
- the Telegram path
- the source list
- the fact that the report is free

## Trust rules
- show the working
- separate sold evidence from asking prices
- sentiment is context only
- cite sources at the foot of every page
- use FAQ to answer the obvious objections
- use real metrics, not fluff

## Routing rules
- SEO article -> valuation CTA
- market timing article -> compare / watchlist / alerts CTA
- compare article -> compare service CTA
- friction article -> partner intro CTA
- city hub -> district report -> Telegram

## Monetisation sequence
1. Free report / free answer
2. Email capture for PDF / next report
3. Telegram start for valuation
4. Compare service for downstream needs
5. Alerts / watchlists / monthly refresh
6. Pro / white-label / partner intros

## What the blog must not do
- hide the Telegram link
- gate the core answer
- use generic CTA text
- stuff keywords
- confuse editorial with product copy
- make the page feel like a brochure
- pretend sentiment is valuation evidence

## Success criteria
- organic traffic to high-intent queries
- CTR to Telegram and compare pages
- email capture rate on lead-gated PDFs
- repeat visits to report pages
- conversion into alerts / watchlists / Pro
- routed partner leads from friction posts
