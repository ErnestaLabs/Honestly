# Honestly — whole-business strategy from the Thiel lecture

## Correction
The blog is not the business.

The postcode-by-postcode network already exists. That is not the final strategy; it is the distribution and evidence layer.

The business is the decision system built on top of that evidence:

> For a specific UK property decision, produce a defensible number, show the evidence, compare the options, monitor the change, and route the next service choice.

## The Thiel lens
A valuable company must do both:

1. Create value.
2. Capture part of the value created.

Honestly creates value by reducing uncertainty in property decisions.

Honestly captures value through:

- paid address-level valuation/report packs
- compare-home decision tools
- alerts/watchlists/monthly monitoring
- partner choice / intro fees where the user picks the winner
- Pro / agent / broker / investor workflows later

If a surface creates trust but does not route to one of those capture points, it is incomplete.

## Existing wedge: postcode-by-postcode evidence
The current postcode reports are already the narrow wedge:

- local district by district
- sold evidence first
- asking prices as context
- live market pace
- exact source framing
- daily refresh
- city hubs from district data

This is good. Preserve it.

But the postcode report is the base layer, not the whole product.

## Real initial market
Do not define the market as:

- UK property content
- property search
- online valuations
- real estate media
- house price news

Those are giant, crowded markets.

Define the initial market as:

> UK buyers and sellers who need to defend one property decision with sold evidence.

Even narrower:

- buyer about to offer on a specific address
- seller deciding whether an asking price is wrong
- buyer comparing two homes
- owner facing a down valuation
- vendor with a stale listing
- investor checking yield and exit risk

These are small markets. They have urgency. They can convert.

## Concentric expansion
Start from the narrow evidence wedge and expand outward.

### Ring 1 — postcode evidence
Free district reports.
Purpose: trust and discovery.
Capture: Telegram valuation CTA.

### Ring 2 — address valuation
One specific address, defensible number, sold comps, confidence, plain English.
Purpose: first paid / Telegram relationship.
Capture: paid report, saved user, follow-up.

### Ring 3 — compare homes
Two or more homes side by side.
Purpose: decision support.
Capture: compare pack, partner intro readiness.

### Ring 4 — alerts / watchlists
Track an address, district, price drop, stuck listing, mortgage-rate change.
Purpose: recurring relationship.
Capture: recurring monitoring.

### Ring 5 — service comparison
Mortgage broker, solicitor, surveyor, insurance, removals, trades.
Purpose: next necessary transaction.
Capture: transparent intro fee from winner, chosen by the user.

### Ring 6 — professional workflows
Agent/vendor pricing, broker evidence packs, investor watchlists, white-label reports.
Purpose: repeated usage.
Capture: subscription / Pro.

## What is the monopoly wedge?
Not “postcode reports.” Others can make pages.

The wedge is:

> The defensible property-decision layer that links local sold evidence to one address, one comparison, one alert, and one next service choice.

The postcode network makes this hard to copy because every product action can point back to a local evidence page.

## What is 10x better?
For the target user, 10x is not “more data.”

10x is:

- one defended number instead of calculator soup
- raw sold evidence instead of black-box valuation
- asking vs sold kept separate
- confidence shown plainly
- compare two homes without a spreadsheet
- alerts after the first answer
- next service choice without being trapped by an agent/broker gate

## Capture discipline
Every major surface must answer:

1. What decision is the user making?
2. What evidence removes uncertainty?
3. What next action captures value?

Examples:

- District report → value this address
- Address valuation → buy full evidence pack / save watchlist
- Compare homes → choose winner / route service need
- Stuck listing → valuation + compare local evidence
- Down valuation → evidence pack + surveyor/broker comparison
- Market headline check → open local report / value address

## What not to do
Do not become a generic publication.
Do not write content because keywords exist.
Do not build “features” without a capture path.
Do not compete with portals on listings breadth.
Do not compete with AVMs on instant black-box confidence.
Do not compete with media on news volume.

## Public copy discipline
Public copy should never expose internal strategy.

Bad public copy:

- authority engine
- content format
- product route
- reader/readers
- monetisation
- funnel
- blog page
- conversion machine

Good public copy:

- Check the sold record.
- Get your address value.
- Compare homes side by side.
- Track price changes.
- See why a listing may be stuck.
- Choose the next service with the evidence in front of you.

## Operating principle
The blog is evidence distribution.
Telegram is the relationship.
Compare is the decision layer.
Alerts are retention.
Partner choice is capture.
Pro is scale.

The business is the loop, not any single page.
