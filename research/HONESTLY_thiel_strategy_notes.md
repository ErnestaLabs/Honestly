# Honestly — Thiel strategy notes

## Correction
This is not a blog strategy.

The blog is one surface. The business is the whole decision loop:

> postcode evidence → address valuation → compare homes → alerts/watchlists → partner choice → Pro workflows

The postcode-by-postcode network already exists. It is the evidence/distribution layer, not the whole company.

## Core lesson
A valuable company must do two things:

1. Create real value.
2. Capture part of the value created.

For Honestly:

- value created = less uncertainty in a property decision
- value captured = paid valuation, comparison, monitoring, partner choice, Pro workflows

Traffic without capture is not enough. Data without a decision is not enough.

## Actual narrow market
Not “UK property content.”
Not “online valuations.”
Not “property search.”

The useful starting market is narrower:

> UK buyers and sellers who need to defend one property decision with sold evidence.

Examples:

- buyer about to make an offer
- seller unsure whether the asking price is wrong
- owner hit by a down valuation
- buyer comparing two homes
- vendor with a stale listing
- investor checking yield and exit risk

## Small market, then expansion
The right expansion path is concentric:

1. Free postcode evidence.
2. Address-level valuation.
3. Side-by-side home comparison.
4. Alerts and watchlists.
5. Broker / solicitor / surveyor / insurance / removals comparison.
6. Pro workflows for repeated users.

Each ring uses the previous ring as evidence.
Each ring must capture value or deepen the relationship.

## Why postcode reports matter
The postcode reports are already the “small market” wedge:

- local
- specific
- official sold evidence
- asking prices clearly separated
- live market pace
- daily updates

They should stay Claude-style: useful, calm, evidence-first, non-meta.

## The monopoly wedge
The defensible wedge is not “pages.”

It is:

> the property-decision layer that connects local sold evidence to one address, one comparison, one alert, and one next service choice.

That is harder to copy than a blog or a calculator.

## 10x improvement
For the user, 10x means:

- one defended number instead of calculator soup
- raw evidence instead of a black box
- compare homes without a spreadsheet
- confidence shown plainly
- follow-up alerts after the first answer
- next service choice without being trapped by an agent or broker gate

## Capture points
Every surface needs a next action.

- postcode report → address valuation
- address valuation → paid evidence pack / save watchlist
- compare homes → choose winner / service need
- stuck listing → valuation + local evidence comparison
- down valuation → evidence pack + broker/surveyor comparison
- headline check → local report / address valuation

## Public copy rule
Public copy serves the decision. It must not explain internal strategy.

Never public-facing:

- authority engine
- content format
- product route
- reader/readers
- monetisation
- funnel
- blog page
- conversion machine

Use instead:

- Check the sold record.
- Get your address value.
- Compare homes side by side.
- Track price changes.
- See why a listing may be stuck.
- Choose the next service with the evidence in front of you.

## One-line strategy
Own the UK property decision moment where a person needs a defensible number and a next action.
