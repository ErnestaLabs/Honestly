# Honestly - page-by-page blog playbook

## Core rule
The blog must own narrow property-decision moments, not generic property content.

A page earns its place only if it:
- answers a specific buyer/seller/investor decision
- shows evidence generic sites do not show clearly
- points to one obvious next action
- captures value through Telegram, comparison, alerts, or partner choice

Start narrow, dominate the decision, then expand outward:
1. local sold record
2. address valuation
3. home comparison
4. alerts / watchlists
5. partner comparison
6. public claims checked against the local record

## Already live
- City hubs for each market
- District reports with sold prices, asking prices, DOM, stuck stock, yield, area intelligence, sentiment, FAQ
- Free PDF lead gate
- Telegram CTA on every city and district page
- Social sentiment blocks labeled as sentiment only
- Source lists and FAQ schema
- Watch cards and market commentary pieces

## What Hit changed
- Move the valuation CTA up on city hubs.
- On money pages, put the primary action first.
- Add mortgage-rate-change alerts as a content cluster.
- Merge "home affordability spreadsheet" into "mortgage-ready comparison".
- Use recurring upsells, not more decorative content.
- Keep the core answer free.
- Use sentiment as enrichment, not evidence.

## 1) City hub page
Example: /blog/city/london/

### Job
Authority + navigation + routing.

### Above the fold
- city headline
- sold-price range
- quick live stats
- short explanation that the figures are official and refreshed daily

### CTA order
1. **Value your own address on Telegram**
2. Browse district reports
3. Follow city updates on Telegram

### Must include
- list of district reports
- short explanation of what each district page contains
- official source framing
- no valuation claim on the blog page itself

### Remove / demote
- any CTA that hides the Telegram path
- generic "read more"
- low-value fluff

### Best use
This page should route visitors into a district page or Telegram.

## 2) District report page
Example: /blog/london-se15/

### Job
Convert local intent.

### Above the fold
- district headline
- sold median
- price per m²
- live asking median
- days on market
- stuck stock

### CTA order
1. **Value your own address on Telegram**
2. Follow district updates on Telegram
3. Email me the free PDF
4. More free reports

### Must include
- sold prices table
- live market table
- yield / rent section if relevant
- area intelligence
- what people say, clearly labelled as sentiment
- FAQ
- official sources

### Notes
- The PDF gate is fine here because the core page remains free.
- The Telegram CTA must stay visible.
- The sentiment block should enrich the appraisal, not pretend to be evidence.

### Best use
This is the best blog page for direct Telegram conversion.

## 3) Market commentary / index page
Examples:
- /blog/london-market-commentary/
- /blog/leeds-market-commentary/
- /blog/uk-city-centre-index/

### Job
Authority, explanation, and inbound links.

Use other people's newsletters, blog posts and market publications as inputs, then compare their claims with the sold record in plain English.
### CTA order
1. **Run the free valuation**
2. Compare options
3. Start a watchlist / get alerts
4. Download the free report

### Best topics
- asking vs sold gap
- why is this house not selling
- city-centre pricing
- down-valued property
- what the market is doing now
- what changed since the last update

### Must include
- clear sold-vs-asking separation
- simple takeaway
- route into the district reports and Telegram

### Remove / demote
- sentiment blocks unless they add something real
- anything that makes the page feel like a generic SEO article

### Best use
This is the page type to use for visibility and trust.

## 4) High-intent friction pages
Topics:
- down-valued property
- why is this house not selling
- mortgage broker gating
- broker / solicitor / surveyor friction
- service charge / leasehold issues

### Job
Capture urgent intent and route to a decision tool.

### CTA order
1. Telegram valuation
2. Compare options
3. Free PDF / lead gate
4. Follow-up newsletter or watchlist

### Why
These users are already in a decision moment.
They need a quick answer and a path to the next decision.

### Best use
These pages should push the compare service hard.

## 5) Compare houses / spreadsheet pages
Topics:
- tools to help compare houses
- price per sqft
- floor plan shapes
- mortgage-ready comparison
- home affordability spreadsheet

### Job
Turn DIY spreadsheet behavior into a product.

### CTA order
1. Compare options
2. Telegram valuation
3. Watchlist / alerts
4. Free PDF

### Rules
- "home affordability spreadsheet" should not stay separate.
- Merge it into "mortgage-ready comparison".
- The point is not a spreadsheet page. The point is a decision page.

### Best use
This cluster feeds the compare service and downstream partner intro flow.

---

## 6) Alerts / watchlist pages
Topics:
- instant alerts
- price drop alerts
- saved search
- watchlist
- market tracker
- mortgage-rate-change alerts

### Job
Recurring revenue.

### CTA order
1. Start free valuation
2. Get alerts / start watchlist
3. Save the property / district
4. Upgrade to recurring tracker

### Why this matters
This is the cleanest recurring hook.
The first answer is free. The ongoing monitoring is paid.

### Best use
Use for buyers, investors, and anyone watching multiple properties.

---

## 7) Lead magnet / PDF gate pages
### Job
Capture email without blocking access to the page.

### Rules
- core page is free
- PDF is gated
- persona field stays in the form
- Telegram CTA stays visible
- no hidden paywall

### CTA order
1. Telegram valuation
2. Email me the free PDF
3. Follow updates / watchlist

### Best use
Use on district pages and especially on high-intent report pages.

---

## 8) Compare service landing pages
### Job
Turn downstream service needs into routed leads.

### Examples
- mortgage brokers
- conveyancers
- surveyors
- insurance
- removals
- utilities
- trades
- staging / cleaning

### CTA order
1. Compare options
2. Pick a winner
3. We make the intro

### Rules
- user chooses the winner
- the lead routing is explicit and opt-in
- the pitch is utility, not sales

### Monetisation
Winner pays for the intro.
This is the goldmine.

## Trust rules
- show the working
- sold evidence first
- asking prices are context only
- sentiment is context only
- cite sources at the foot of every page
- use FAQ to answer the obvious objections
- use real numbers, not generic claims

---

## What to cut
- decorative sentiment blocks on low-intent pages
- watch cards where there is no alerts CTA
- generic "download free report" on city hubs
- keyword stuffing
- any page that feels like a content farm

---

## Operating model
Blog -> authority -> Telegram -> compare service -> alerts / watchlist -> partner intro -> Pro / white-label.

Every page has one job.
No inch is decorative.
